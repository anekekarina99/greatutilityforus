FAQ:
- What's the formula for compound interest with monthly contributions?
  It combines two parts: the future value of your initial amount `A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)`, plus the future value of a series of deposits `PMT × [((1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1) / (r/n)]`.
- Should contributions be added at the start or end of each month?
  Most calculators assume end-of-month by default. Adding at the start grows slightly faster because each deposit compounds one extra period.
- Is monthly contribution better than a one-time lump sum?
  They serve different goals. A lump sum compounds longer, but consistent monthly deposits build the habit and add more total principal over time.
- Does the interest rate change my results a lot?
  Yes. Because contributions compound repeatedly, even a 1–2% difference in rate creates a large gap over 10–20 years.
